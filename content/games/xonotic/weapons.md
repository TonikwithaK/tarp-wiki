# Weapons

Xonotic has 9 core weapons. Players spawn with Blaster and Shotgun; all others are map pickups. The Blaster has no ammo cost and doubles as the primary movement tool.

Source: Halogene's Newbie Corner (https://xonotic.org/guide/)

## Blaster

No ammo cost. Low damage. Fires a projectile that creates a blast on impact, pushing anything nearby away from the impact point.

Primary use is movement (see [[games/xonotic/movement|Movement]]). Secondary use: displacing opponents, breaking movement, pushing players off edges on space maps. Aim at feet for splash on moving targets. Secondary fire switches to previous weapon.

## Shotgun

Spawn weapon. High damage up close via instant-hit pellet spread. Push force slows targets moving toward you — useful against players descending from jump pads. Secondary: melee slap, deals significant damage at point-blank.

## MachineGun

Also called "uzi." Primary: high spread, close to medium range. Secondary: burst fire, lower spread, medium to long range. Push force plus rapid fire is effective at stopping fast-approaching players. A ring around the crosshair tracks ammo; the gun auto-reloads when empty. Re-press the weapon bind while holding it to manually reload at a convenient time.

## Mortar

Ballistic grenade launcher. High direct and splash damage plus push force. Primary detonates on impact. Secondary detonates exactly 0.5 seconds after the first bounce — this is precise enough to time deliberately. Strong against non-flying targets and for spamming corners.

## Electro

Primary: rocket-like projectile, not useful for self-jumps. Secondary: fires up to 3 slow-moving blobs. Detonate the blobs with a primary fire hit for ~130 damage — works via splash, so hitting a surface near the blobs is sufficient. Can set off through walls (place blobs around a corner, hit the wall beyond them with primary). Best used for entry denial, multi-opponent clusters, or predictive area spam.

## Crylink

Unique: negative push force. Hitting a player with Crylink slows them dramatically regardless of movement direction. Multiple particles landing simultaneously can stop a player at full speed. Widely used by CTF defenders to shut down flag runners.

Primary has high spread, but releases the spread inversion mechanic: while holding primary, spread is constant. When you release primary, all particles converge to a single point. Time the release so the convergence lands on the target.

Secondary: low constant spread, faster particles, no bounce. Can break trick jumps or pull players in mid-air via negative push. Movement application ("Crylink running"): just as you leave the ground on your first bunny hop, shoot the floor at ~45° in front of you with secondary — the negative push catapults you forward. Also enables wall climbing: face a wall, jump, shoot the wall above your head with secondary.

## Vortex

Hitscan beam, zero spread, shoots through players. Charges automatically while held (no ammo cost). Charge ring around crosshair is visible; full charge glows red. Full charge: 80 dmg. Immediate second shot: 76 dmg. Minimum (switch away immediately after firing and switch back): 59 dmg. Secondary only zooms. Most powerful weapon in capable hands.

## Hagar

Rapid-fire rockets, no spread. Primary: continuous. Secondary: hold to load up to 4 rockets, release to fire in a three-point star pattern. Holding too long triggers an automatic release with a warning beep. High close-range damage from the volley, but significant splash self-damage risk. No-spread makes it viable at range despite slow projectile travel. On most competitive servers: press primary while holding secondary to cancel the load without firing.

## Devastator

Remote detonation: secondary fire immediately detonates any in-flight rocket. No need to directly hit a target — get it close and detonate. Guided rockets: hold primary and move mouse to steer in flight. Combine guidance and detonation.

High push force on explosion — best tool for knocking airborne players off maps.

Self-boost ("rocket flying"): while airborne, fire a rocket downward and immediately detonate it. Deals heavy self-damage but can save you from the void or extend a Blaster jump to reach a distant platform.

## Weapon Comboing

Weapon switch delay is shorter than any weapon's refire time. Switching immediately after firing lets you land a second hit faster than waiting for the same weapon to refire, increasing effective damage per second. Also lets you stack effects — e.g., Crylink to stop a fast target, then Vortex to finish.

Direct weapon key binds are strongly recommended; mouse wheel and default layout make reliable comboing nearly impossible. Some players remap movement from WASD to ESDF to free up nearby keys.
