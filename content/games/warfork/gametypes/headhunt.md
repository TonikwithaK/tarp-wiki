# Headhunt

Headhunt is a free-for-all mode built around a shifting target. One player is designated as the head. All other players hunt them. The head scores points for surviving. When the head is killed, the player who landed the kill becomes the new head: and the hunt resumes with a new target.

There are no permanent teams. Alliances are meaningless. The player who holds the head the longest wins.

---

## Core Loop

The mode rotates through three distinct states for any given player:

**As the head:** You're the target. Every other player on the server has incentive to kill you. You score continuously while alive as the head: the longer you survive, the more points you accumulate. Dying transfers the head to the player who killed you and stops your scoring.

**As a hunter:** You're trying to kill the current head before anyone else does. The kill matters for two reasons: it gives you the head, and it denies the current head further points. But be aware that the moment you become the head, you inherit the same pressure: every other hunter will immediately pivot to you.

**As a fresh spawn after dying:** Re-enter the map, reorient, and get back into position to hunt or to contest the head if the kill opportunity presents itself.

---

## Playing as the Head

Survival requires evasive [[movement]] above all else. You are outnumbered from the first second. Staying alive means never stopping, never holding still, and using the full geometry of the map: verticality, teleporters, sightline breaks: to make yourself a difficult target.

The instinct to fight back hard is often counterproductive. You can and should fight to protect yourself, but a prolonged stationary duel lets other hunters converge on your position. Stay mobile. Break line of sight. Force hunters to commit to chasing you through terrain that puts them at risk from other players.

Knowing the map pays dividends here more than in almost any other mode. A head who knows where teleporters exit, which corridors are dead ends, and which routes loop back to open space can survive far longer than raw mechanical skill alone would allow.

---

## Playing as a Hunter

Hunting the head is competitive, not cooperative. Every other hunter is simultaneously your rival. The kill goes to exactly one player, and that player takes the head: so coordinating with other hunters to pressure the target doesn't help you unless you're the one landing the final shot.

This creates a tension between chasing down the current head efficiently and holding a position that lets you intercept the head as other hunters push them toward you. Experienced players think about where the head is likely to move rather than just where they currently are.

After a kill, immediately expect to become the target. The transition from hunter to head is the most dangerous moment in Headhunt. You've just revealed your position to the entire server.

---

## Item Pickups and Loadout

Item pickups are available on the map in standard fashion. Spawn loadout and starting health and armor are tuned for the fast-paced nature of the mode: fights resolve quickly, and the tempo of head transfers is high.

Controlling health and armor spawns gives you staying power as the head. If you can collect significant items while staying in motion, you extend how long you survive each stint as the target.

---

## Why It's Worth Playing

Headhunt forces adaptability. You can't lock into one playstyle: the mode demands that you switch between evasion and aggression on the fly, read the entire server simultaneously rather than focusing on one opponent, and make rapid decisions about when to fight and when to run. These are skills that transfer broadly.

It is also chaotic in a way that other modes aren't, which makes it a low-pressure environment to develop map awareness and movement under fire.
