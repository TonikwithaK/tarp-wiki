# Deathmatch

Deathmatch is free-for-all. Every player on the server is your opponent. The winner is whoever reaches the frag limit first, or whoever holds the highest frag count when the time limit expires. No teams, no objectives, no coordination tax: just you, the map, and everyone else trying to kill you.

It is the most accessible way into Warfork's combat.

---

## How It Works

Players spawn with a starting loadout. Weapons, health packs, and armor are distributed across the map as standard pickups. You acquire better equipment by picking it up. You respawn quickly after dying, re-entering the map at a spawn point and getting back into the fight almost immediately.

The pace is high and continuous. Unlike round-based modes, death doesn't end your round: it just sets you back a moment. The accumulated frag count is what matters, and the match doesn't stop until someone hits the limit or time runs out.

---

## Positioning and Opportunism

In FFA, everyone is a potential target and everyone is a potential threat. Playing thoughtfully means knowing when to engage and when to let two enemies damage each other before moving in. An opponent at 20 health after a skirmish is a free frag; diving into a 3-way fight simultaneously is a good way to die without accomplishing much.

Positioning in Deathmatch is largely about being where frags happen while not being exposed to the entire server at once. High-traffic weapon and item areas generate engagements. Controlling them means frags come to you. Standing in the open middle of the map means frags come to you for the wrong reason.

---

## Item Pickups and Economy

Weapons, health, and armor spawn at fixed locations and on timers. Players who know the map can route through pickup locations efficiently: collecting health and weapons between engagements rather than finding themselves weak and under-armed mid-match.

There's no item denial dynamic as intense as Duel's because the server population means multiple players are always competing for the same resources. Still, knowing where the significant armor and health spawns are: and arriving before your opponents: creates a sustained advantage.

---

## Deathmatch vs. Other Modes

Deathmatch has lower structural complexity than [[duel]] or [[clan-arena]]. There's no item timing to master, no team coordination required, and no round structure to internalize. That makes it accessible but also relatively shallow at the top end: the ceiling for what coordination and strategy can add is lower when there's no team and no shared objective.

That said, Deathmatch sharpens raw combat: tracking moving targets, managing your health economy while under threat from multiple directions, and making quick decisions about whether to push or disengage. These skills transfer directly to every other mode.

For players coming from other games, Deathmatch is the right starting point. It exposes you to Warfork's weapons, movement, and pace without asking you to learn mode-specific rulesets first.

---

## Team Deathmatch

[[team-deathmatch]] is a related but distinct mode. The core scoring logic is the same: most frags wins: but players are divided into teams and individual frags contribute to a shared team score. Coordination and map control matter more in TDM because your team's collective output, not just yours, determines the result.
