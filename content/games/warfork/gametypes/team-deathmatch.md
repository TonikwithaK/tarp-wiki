# Team Deathmatch

Team Deathmatch (TDM) is a team-based frag race. Players are divided into two teams. Every kill any player lands scores for their team. The team with the highest combined frag count when the time limit expires, or the first to hit the frag limit, wins.

Respawning is continuous: there are no rounds, no elimination, no waiting in spectator. Death costs you a moment and a point from your team's total if someone kills you, but the match continues regardless.

---

## How It Differs from Clan Arena

The comparison to [[clan-arena]] comes up constantly, and the distinction matters.

In Clan Arena, the round ends when a team is fully eliminated. Surviving is a win condition: the last player standing on a team contributes directly to winning that round by staying alive. Respawning doesn't exist within a round.

In TDM, survival isn't a win condition. Frags are. A player who dies but was responsible for two kills during that life contributed positively to the team's total. A player who hides safely all match and racks up zero frags is a dead weight, even if they never die. The mode rewards consistent, high-output play over cautious play.

This also changes the risk calculus on every engagement. In CA, dying when you're the last alive loses the round. In TDM, dying is a setback measured in score, not a round-ending event.

## Map Control and Team Coordination

Standard item pickups: weapons, health, armor: are present on the map and available to both teams. The team that controls the significant armor and weapon spawns will consistently win engagements, because their players are better equipped. This is the same dynamic as in [[deathmatch]], but the team structure amplifies it.

If two players from your team hold the armor room and rotate through its pickups, neither of them will be fighting at a deficit. The opposing team, fighting without armor, will lose engagements at the margins and fall behind in frags. Map control is sustainable frag advantage.

Coordination in TDM doesn't require the tight synchronization of CA. You don't need to time a coordinated push or maintain strict group positioning for every round. But playing near teammates: fighting 2v1 where possible, trading fire rather than diving alone into 1v2 situations: will reliably outperform lone-wolf play over the course of a match.

---

## Practical Play

The most common error in TDM is playing it like FFA Deathmatch: each player routing independently, picking whatever fights happen to be nearby, ignoring teammates. This produces roughly the same output as your individual FFA skill level, and it's exploitable by a team that plays with any coordination at all.

The most common correction is simple: stay in loose proximity to at least one teammate during fights. A 2v1 anywhere on the map is a near-certain frag and usually a safe trade. Engineering those situations consistently, while denying the same to the other team, is what TDM coordination actually looks like in practice.

Item awareness matters. Know where the major health and armor spawns are. Know when they respawn. Route through them.
