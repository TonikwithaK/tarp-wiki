# Race

Race is a movement-focused game mode with no combat. Players cannot deal damage to each other. The objective is to complete a course in the fastest possible time.

## What Race Is

There are no weapons to worry about, no health to manage, no opponents to fight. The only variable is you and the course. Race strips the game down to its movement system and asks you to execute it as cleanly as possible across a designed obstacle layout.

Your best completion time is recorded. The mode is built around improving that time and competing with others' times on the same map. Leaderboards give you an objective benchmark — you are always chasing a specific number.

## Course Design

Race maps are purpose-built around Warfork's movement toolkit. They are not repurposed combat maps. A well-designed race course will require you to chain techniques — strafejumping to build and maintain speed, walljumps to clear vertical sections or redirect momentum, plasmaclimbing to reach positions that ground movement cannot reach, and precise air control to stay on narrow paths at high velocity.

Some race maps include item pickups that provide speed boosts or other advantages along the route. These are part of the course design, not incidental.

Race map names follow the convention: `wf` prefix with `race` in the name (e.g., `wfrace_mapname`). If you're browsing the server browser or a map list, this naming pattern identifies them quickly.

## Plasmaclimbing in Race

Plasmaclimbing — using Plasma Gun shots beneath you to boost upward — is especially relevant in race. Many race routes include sections that cannot be completed without it. If you are working through a race map and find yourself stuck at a vertical obstacle with no apparent path, plasmaclimbing is likely the intended solution.

## Respawning and Checkpoints

You can respawn at any time to restart your run from the beginning or from a checkpoint if the map uses them. There is no penalty for resetting — the expectation is that you will run the course many times, incrementally improving your time and refining specific sections that cost you.

## Race as Movement Training

Race is one of the most effective ways to develop movement fundamentals. Because there is no combat pressure, you can focus entirely on technique — on reading your strafe HUD, on timing your jumps, on maintaining momentum through turns. The feedback loop is tight: run the course, see your time, identify what slowed you down, fix it.

For pure movement practice outside of race, `wfca1` is a community-recognized practice map that covers movement basics, though it is not a race map itself.

If you want to get better at [[movement]] in general, race is the training ground that rewards precision most directly and most measurably.
