# Duel

Duel is 1v1. First player to the frag limit wins, or whoever has more frags when the time limit expires. There are no teammates to carry you, no spawn advantage over a group, no lucky team wipe to swing momentum. Every outcome is directly yours.

It is the most mechanically demanding mode in Warfork.

---

## Starting Conditions

Players spawn with a Machine Gun and a modest supply of initial health and armor. Neither player arrives with the full weapon set. That means the opening seconds of a duel are not just about surviving — they're about routing to the weapons and items you need before your opponent can.

The full arsenal becomes available only through map pickups. Getting the Rocket Launcher first, or denying your opponent access to it, is already a meaningful advantage.

---

## Item Control

At high levels, Duel is primarily a game of item control. Health packs, armor shards, and — most critically — the Mega Health and major armor pickups are the engine that keeps you in the fight. These items respawn on fixed timers. Knowing when each one comes back is what separates players who fight from equal or disadvantaged positions from players who consistently engineer favorable ones.

The player who controls the Mega Health and heavy armor will, over the course of a match, have more health, more armor, and more sustained fighting capacity. They don't have to win every duel — they can afford to absorb more mistakes. The player who falls behind on item timing has to gamble on engagements just to break even.

**Tracking item timers is not optional at intermediate and above levels.** There are two common approaches: mental tracking (counting seconds after a pickup is taken) and auditory cues from item-related sound effects. Either works. Neither is easy under fire, which is why building the habit matters.

---

## Map Control

Map control and item control are the same thing expressed differently. When you're moving through the map with purpose — denying your opponent angles, holding the rooms that contain the best items, funneling your opponent through predictable routes — you are simultaneously building your resource advantage and shaping where fights happen.

The player who controls the map decides when and where most engagements occur. If that's you, you can take fights on your terms. If it's your opponent, you're reacting.

This doesn't mean being passive. Aggressive item denial — moving to cut off an opponent from a pickup before they get there — is map control. So is clearing a room, establishing area presence, and forcing the opponent into a suboptimal position.

---

## Combat Mechanics

Strafe-jumping is the movement baseline. You need to be moving quickly and unpredictably in every fight. A stationary or slow-moving target in Warfork is a dead target.

Strafe dodging — quick direction changes in the air to make yourself harder to track — is what keeps you alive against good aim. The goal is not to be evasive in a random, flailing sense, but to move in ways that are unpredictable to the opponent while remaining controlled to you.

The Rocket Launcher is the dominant weapon at mid-range. Learning to land rockets on a strafing opponent, and to dodge rockets while returning fire, is the central mechanical skill of Duel. Direct hits win fights quickly; missed rockets are a health and ammo cost that compounds over time.

---

## Duel Arena Variant

Duel Arena is a queue-based, informal variant typically run on community servers. The differences:

- Players spawn with all weapons rather than acquiring them through pickups
- The winner stays; the next player in the queue enters
- First to 11 frags wins the match

This removes item timing as a variable and turns the mode into a pure combat test. It's a good format for practicing the mechanical side of dueling — tracking, dodging, weapon switching — without the overhead of map memorization and timer tracking. However, it rewards a different skill set than standard Duel, so don't treat Duel Arena performance as a direct proxy for your Duel ranking.

---

## Improvement Path

New to Duel: Focus on surviving long enough to acquire weapons. Learn the map. Understand where the Mega Health and armor are located.

Intermediate: Start tracking one item timer — just the Mega Health. Show up before it respawns and take it. Build this into habit before adding more.

Advanced: Track multiple items simultaneously. Deny items proactively. Use item timing information to predict where your opponent is moving.

Competitive: Combine item control with active map denial. Force fights when you have the health and armor advantage. Disengage when you don't.
