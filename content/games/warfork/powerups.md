# Powerups

Powerups are temporary enhancements that spawn at fixed locations on fixed timers. They are the highest-value pickups on any map that has them: and like health and armor, controlling them is as much about denial as it is about personal benefit.

## The Three Powerups

**Quad Damage** multiplies your damage output for its duration. Every weapon becomes significantly more lethal. A player carrying Quad can trade favorably in almost any engagement: even a weak hit punishes harder than normal. Forcing a fight while carrying Quad, or avoiding one while the opponent has it, is a core tactical decision.

**WarShell** reduces incoming damage for its duration. Where Quad makes you more dangerous, WarShell makes you harder to kill. A player with WarShell active can absorb punishment that would otherwise end a fight quickly, enabling aggressive pushes into contested areas or surviving situations that would normally be fatal.

**Regeneration** heals HP over time for its duration. It does not provide immediate burst healing, but it sustains you through a stretch of play: useful for recovering between engagements without having to hunt health items, and for keeping your HP topped off while holding a strong map position.

## Spawn Timing and Map Control

Powerups spawn at fixed map locations on fixed respawn timers, following the same logic as Mega Health and Yellow Armor. Once a powerup is picked up, the timer resets and you know when it will return. High-level players track these timers as part of their mental map awareness.

The denial calculus applies directly: a Quad Damage that your opponent cannot reach is worth more than one you use carelessly. If you're in a strong position and can intercept a powerup spawn, do so even if you don't need the effect. Leaving it for a desperate opponent to collect is a losing trade.

## Timing as a Skill

Powerup timing is the same discipline as item timing in [[duel]]: you're managing a mental clock for multiple high-value spawns simultaneously, while also managing a fight. Players who can hold a spawn point for a powerup while tracking the opponent's position have already won part of the information war.

If you're losing a match, denying powerups is one of the few ways to slow the resource bleed. If you're winning, powerups extend your lead and can force concession of entire map sections.
