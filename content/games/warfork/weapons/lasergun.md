# Lasergun

The Lasergun is the shaft equivalent in Warfork: the primary tracking weapon and the mid-range pillar of the competitive weapon triad alongside the Rocket Launcher and Electrobolt. Mastering it requires sustained aim, ammo discipline, and knowing when to switch off.

## Stats

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Damage | 7 per tick |
| Fire Rate | 50ms per tick (~140 DPS sustained) |
| Self-Damage | 0 |
| Knockback | 14 per tick |
| Stun | 300 per tick |
| Splash Radius | None |
| Range | Long |

## Mechanics

The Lasergun fires a continuous hitscan beam that connects instantly: no travel time, no projectile to lead. As long as the beam is touching the target, it deals damage at a rate of one tick every 50ms. Fully connected over one second, that is 140 DPS. The beam has perfect accuracy: it does not spread, deviate, or degrade over distance. Whatever you point it at, it hits.

Each tick applies 14 knockback and 300 stun to the target. The knockback accumulates with every tick, pushing opponents off their intended trajectory and disrupting their air control. The stun value is high: 300 per tick is enough to interrupt actions repeatedly if you hold the beam on target.

There is no self-damage. You cannot rocket-jump with a Lasergun.

## Ammo

Ammo consumption is the Lasergun's primary constraint in practice. At a tick every 50ms, sustained fire burns through ammunition fast. Firing into open air: missing a moving target and holding the trigger anyway: is one of the easiest ways to run dry at a critical moment. The habit to develop is letting go when the beam is clearly not connecting, rather than holding out hoping the target walks back into it.

Manage pickups accordingly. Lasergun ammo on the map should be treated as a priority alongside health and armor, particularly in duel formats where ammo denial is part of map control.

## Role in Competitive Play

In standard competitive modes: duel, Clan Arena, team formats: the Lasergun is the mid-range damage weapon. It covers the gap between the Rocket Launcher's effective close-to-mid range and the Electrobolt's long-range precision role. When a fight is happening at a distance where rockets are difficult to land cleanly and too far for the Riotgun, the Lasergun is the correct weapon.

The LG does not operate in isolation. The standard competitive weapon set is RL for close-to-mid engagements, LG for mid, and EB for long. Knowing which weapon to be on at any moment: and switching quickly: separates effective players from those who over-rely on one tool.

## Aim Requirement

The Lasergun demands tracking aim. You are not making a single clean flick at a target; you are keeping a beam on a moving player across a sustained period. The target will strafe, jump, change direction, and do everything possible to break your beam. Your job is to follow them.

This is a different skill set from the Electrobolt's flick-and-release pattern. Lasergun accuracy is measured over time rather than per shot: a good LG player maintains a high percentage of ticks on target through continuous adjustment. The knockback each tick applies to the target also makes them harder to track as the fight progresses, since it pushes them off predictable paths.

## Counters

Breaking line of sight is the most direct counter. The Lasergun cannot deal damage through walls, and an opponent who ducks behind geometry forces you off the weapon entirely.

Unpredictable movement: irregular jumps, direction changes, using terrain to create partial cover: degrades your tick percentage and reduces effective DPS. At close range, the Rocket Launcher is generally more efficient: the splash radius means less precision is needed, and the damage per shot outweighs what the LG can deal in the same time frame at that distance.

An opponent running directly at you while you fire the Lasergun is easier to track than one moving perpendicular or erratically. Closing the range and switching to rockets is the correct response when you recognize a player is trying to hold the LG on you.
