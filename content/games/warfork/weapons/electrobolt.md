# Electrobolt

The Electrobolt is the rail equivalent in Warfork: a single-shot, long-range hitscan weapon with the highest per-shot damage of any standard weapon and the longest cooldown in the game. It is the third pillar of the RL/LG/EB competitive triad, covering the range where rockets miss and the Lasergun struggles to track.

## Stats

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Damage | 75 |
| Fire Rate | 1250ms cooldown |
| Self-Damage | 0 |
| Knockback | 80 |
| Stun | 1000 |
| Splash Radius | None |
| Travel Time | Instant (hitscan) |

## Mechanics

The Electrobolt fires a single hitscan beam that connects instantly at the moment of firing. There is no projectile, no travel time, and no lead required: if the crosshair is on the target when you pull the trigger, the shot lands. A miss is equally immediate: the beam fires, connects nothing, and you wait 1250ms before you can fire again.

At 75 damage per shot, a clean Electrobolt hit removes a large portion of any opponent's health. Paired with the 80 knockback and 1000 stun on impact, a hit does not just deal damage: it disrupts the opponent's movement significantly. The 1000 stun value is among the highest in the game. An opponent struck by the Electrobolt will feel their trajectory interrupted, which affects their ability to dodge follow-up shots or execute movement techniques immediately after.

There is no self-damage.

## The 1250ms Cooldown

The Electrobolt's cooldown is what defines its risk profile. 1250ms is the longest gap between shots of any weapon in Warfork. A miss is not just a missed opportunity: it is a window. An opponent who sees or hears you fire and miss knows they have over a second to act: close distance, reposition, push through a doorway, or launch a rocket jump toward you. A player who respects the Electrobolt when it fires and discards that respect the instant it misses is the correct opponent to play against.

This asymmetry demands shot discipline. The correct instinct is not to fire on any opportunity, but to fire on clean opportunities. Forcing shots through heavy movement or partial cover: hoping to clip the target: is a lower-percentage approach than waiting for a moment of predictable positioning. Every miss costs more than just ammo.

## Role in Competitive Play

The Electrobolt handles the long-range engagement where the other two weapons in the triad are less efficient. Rockets travel slowly and are straightforward to dodge at range: a player with any movement knowledge will avoid direct hits at long distance. The Lasergun's beam requires sustained tracking, which at long range means fighting over a much larger area of potential target movement.

The Electrobolt requires only one tick of aim: the moment you fire. At range, targets move more slowly across your screen relative to their actual speed, making the tracking challenge smaller. Chokepoints, teleporter exits, and open sight lines with no cover are where the Electrobolt is most valuable: positions where the opponent has to cross your crosshair to reach you.

In Clan Arena, the Electrobolt is critical for picking targets across open spaces and covering teleporter exits. With no respawns, each hit at full EB damage is permanent progress. Players in CA who hold long sight lines with the Electrobolt force opponents to use cover or movement that the rest of their team can exploit.

## Aim Requirement

The Electrobolt rewards two distinct aim skills depending on context. At true long range, the shot is often a flick: you identify a moment when the target's movement is predictable, bring the crosshair to them, and fire. At medium-long range, some tracking is involved: following the target's movement and releasing when the crosshair alignment is correct.

Both approaches demand that you fire when the shot is actually good, not when you are impatient. The cooldown punishes impatience more than any other mechanic in the game.

## Counters

The direct counter to the Electrobolt is the cooldown window. An opponent who closes distance during the 1250ms after a shot forces the Electrobolt user onto a weapon less suited to the new range, or demands that they make a difficult medium-range EB shot under pressure.

Erratic movement: irregular jump patterns, sudden direction changes: degrades tracking at range and makes the shot harder to time. Breaking line of sight removes the threat entirely. Against a player relying heavily on the EB, finding routes that offer cover between positions, rather than crossing open ground, reduces their ability to pick you cleanly.
