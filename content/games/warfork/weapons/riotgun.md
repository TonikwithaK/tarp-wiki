# Riotgun

The Riotgun is a close-range burst weapon that fires 20 hitscan pellets in a conical spread. At point blank, it deals up to 100 damage in a single shot — the highest single-shot burst in standard gameplay. Its effective range is short, its cooldown is punishing, and its threat drops off fast with distance. Used correctly, it opens fights and closes them at close range. Used carelessly, it hands your opponent a free engagement.

## Stats

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Damage | 5 per pellet × 20 pellets (100 max) |
| Fire Rate | 900ms cooldown |
| Self-Damage | 0 |
| Knockback | 7 per pellet |
| Stun | 85 |
| Spread | Conical |
| Splash Radius | None |

## Mechanics

Each shot fires 20 pellets simultaneously in a cone. All pellets are hitscan — they register instantly on whatever geometry or player they intersect. At point-blank range, the cone is narrow enough that most or all pellets connect with a standing target, delivering up to 100 damage. The 85 stun on a full or near-full hit disrupts the opponent's movement noticeably, creating an opening for follow-up.

As distance increases, the cone expands. Fewer pellets land, damage falls off, and the stun effect diminishes proportionally. At medium range, the Riotgun becomes unreliable. At long range, it is nearly useless.

There is no self-damage.

## Not a Spray Weapon

The 900ms cooldown is the defining constraint. You fire once and wait almost a full second before you can fire again. That gap is long enough for an opponent to reposition, counter-attack, or exploit the opening completely.

This means the Riotgun is not a weapon you fire at a general area and hope for the best. Each shot is a commitment. If you are not confident the target is inside the cone at close range, the correct play is often to wait or switch weapons rather than fire and waste the shot — and the 900ms that follows it.

## Role

The Riotgun's primary role is close-range burst damage. Walking into a room and finding an opponent at 5–10 meters is a good Riotgun situation. The burst potential at 100 damage is high enough to heavily wound or kill a weakened opponent in a single shot, and the stun creates a window to finish them with the Lasergun or Rocket Launcher.

It is also effective as an opener in close-quarters engagements. A Riotgun shot followed immediately by LG beam or RL rockets can combine the burst damage with the sustained or splash damage of the follow-up weapon. The stun from the Riotgun helps ensure the follow-up lands.

The Riotgun does not belong in mid or long range engagements. If the fight has moved outside its effective cone, switch weapons.

## Counters

Circle-strafing is the most reliable mechanical counter. Moving perpendicular to the shooter means the cone has to rotate to follow you — if you can step out of its angle before the shot fires, you take reduced or no damage. The 900ms cooldown means the Riotgun user has to track your movement accurately at the moment of firing, not just be pointed in your direction.

Jumping is also effective. The vertical displacement changes the angle relative to a standing shooter's aim, and many players do not adjust well to tracking vertical movement with the Riotgun.

Unpredictable movement in general makes the Riotgun harder to land. The weapon only pays off when the shot is clean, and any movement that creates uncertainty for the shooter during that 900ms window degrades their accuracy.

At longer ranges, simply maintaining distance is sufficient. The Riotgun cannot threaten you effectively if you do not close to its effective range.
