# Instagib

Instagib is a modifier, not a standalone game mode. It can be layered onto most gametypes — Instagib FFA, Instagib CTF, and others. What it does is replace every player's loadout with a single weapon: the Instagun.

## The Instagun

The Instagun fires an instant-kill hitscan beam. It has infinite ammo. One hit kills, regardless of the target's health or armor. There is no resource management, no weapon switching, no health pickups that matter. Every engagement comes down entirely to aim.

The Instagun's demand on precision is comparable to the [[electrobolt]] — you are placing an exact shot on a moving target. The key difference is that the Instagun fires faster, which tightens the timing of exchanges. A miss costs you very little in terms of ammo; the penalty is purely the time you gave the opponent to return fire or reposition.

## Movement as Defense

With one-shot kills and no health buffer, movement is the only defense available. Standing still or moving predictably is a death sentence against a player with decent aim. The correct response is constant strafing, air control, and keeping yourself in unpredictable positions.

This is where Instagib becomes a training mode as much as a game mode. Because survival is entirely dependent on how hard you are to hit, it forces you to internalize movement principles under live fire pressure. Players who struggle to strafe consistently in normal play often find Instagib corrects that quickly.

See [[movement]] for the techniques that directly improve your survivability here.

## Instagib Modifiers

Instagib servers may enable additional modifiers on top of the base mode:

**Instashield** grants temporary invulnerability. It is typically used for aggressive pushes — crossing open ground or contesting a position that would otherwise be suicidal. Timing the shield well is a skill in itself.

**Instajump** enhances jump height and speed, opening up movement routes and making you harder to track during vertical repositioning. Maps that support Instajump often have geometry that rewards it.

**Allow Self Damage** toggles whether a player can harm themselves with their own Instagun beam. This affects positioning decisions near walls and close-quarters geometry. When enabled, you need to account for beam angles relative to your own body.

## Applying Instagib to a Server

The console command to enable Instagib exists, but specifics vary by server configuration and whether you have the relevant admin access. If you're hosting your own server, check your server config documentation for the correct cvar. If you're looking for public Instagib games, filter the server browser by game modifier.
