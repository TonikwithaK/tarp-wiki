# Capture the Flag — Warfork

Capture the Flag is a team-based game mode in which two teams compete to steal the enemy flag from their base and return it to their own. It is one of the more tactically layered modes in Warfork, combining individual combat skill with team coordination, map knowledge, and movement routing.

## Core Rules

Each team has a flag planted at their base. To score a point, a player must pick up the enemy flag, carry it across the map, and touch their own team's flag at their base. A capture only registers if your own flag is present — if the enemy is holding your flag, you cannot score until it is returned. Dropped flags return to base after a timer.

The team that reaches the score limit — or holds the highest score at time limit — wins the match.

Friendly fire is enabled in CTF. Teammates can damage each other, which matters both in chaotic base fights and when covering a flag carrier with explosive weapons.

## Map Structure

CTF maps are designed around two distinct bases with multiple routes connecting them. The layout typically provides options: a main corridor, secondary paths, vertical routes, and shortcuts. Knowing these routes is not optional knowledge — it directly determines how effective you are as a flag runner and how well you can defend against incoming attackers.

Maps also distribute item pickups — health, armor, and weapons — across the field. Controlling high-value items on route to the enemy base or near your own flag benefits both attackers and defenders. Teams that understand item timing on a specific map have a structural advantage over teams reacting to fights on the fly.

## Roles

CTF play generally breaks into two responsibilities: offense and defense. In practice, most teams will send dedicated attackers toward the enemy flag while one or more players stay back to hold the home base.

**Flag runners** prioritize reaching the enemy flag and escaping with it. Speed, movement efficiency, and route knowledge are the core skills. A flag carrier who can walljump and strafe through a map efficiently gives defenders less time to react and intercepts less fire than one moving slowly along predictable lines. The flag carrier cannot use weapons while dead — survival is the objective, not winning every fight.

**Defenders** hold the home base against attackers and return the flag if it is dropped. A defender needs to know the angles into the flag room, the timing of incoming attackers, and how to deal with multiple opponents at once. Positioning and item control near the base determine how hard it is for attackers to extract the flag.

Both roles demand combat competence. A flag carrier who cannot fight at all will be killed on their way out of the enemy base. A defender who loses every duel cannot hold the flag room.

## Movement and Route Knowledge

Movement skill is more directly valuable in CTF than in most other modes. A flag runner using walljumps and strafing to maximize their speed across the map is genuinely harder to intercept than one running in a straight line. Route knowledge compounds this — knowing which path to take, when to take it, and what items to grab on the way is a significant edge.

The ability to escape a fight without dying is particularly important for flag carriers. Knowing when to disengage, use vertical movement to break pursuit, or take a less contested route back to base can decide whether a capture happens or the flag is dropped for recovery. ## Variants

**Instagib CTF** replaces all weapons with the Instagun — one-hit kills for all players. The pace of the mode changes significantly: individual combat becomes binary (hit or miss), and team coordination around flag movement becomes the dominant skill.

**Class-based CTF** introduces distinct player classes with different capabilities, changing the role structure considerably. See [[class-based-ctf]] for details.
