# Health and Armor

Health and armor form the resource layer of Warfork's combat. Understanding their values, interactions, and timers separates players who survive fights from players who control matches.

## Health

Your base maximum health is 100 HP. Most health items restore up to that cap. Some items push you above it — this is called overhealing. Overhealed HP decays back down to 100 over time; it does not stay permanently.

### Health Items

| Item | HP Restored | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Health Bubble | 5 HP | Small pickup scattered across maps, often used to patch minor damage |
| Small Health | 25 HP | Moderate restore, useful for topping off between fights |
| Large Health | 50 HP | Significant restore, worth controlling in tighter maps |
| Mega Health | 100 HP | Restores to full; can push you above 100 HP (overheals) |
| Ultra Health | 200 HP | Major overheal; may be Warfork-specific — verify availability on your server or ruleset |

Overhealed health decays gradually back to 100. There is no benefit to sitting on an overheal — keep moving and use the advantage before it bleeds off.

### HUD Feedback

The health display on your HUD changes color as your HP changes. This gives you a fast read on your current state without needing to focus on the number itself. Learn to register these color shifts in peripheral vision during combat.

### Simple Items

The "Simple Items" display setting replaces 3D pickup models with flat 2D sprites. This is a performance and readability option — it does not change pickup values or behavior in any way.

## Armor

Armor Points (AP) act as a damage buffer. When you take damage, AP is consumed first. Only after your AP is depleted does damage start reducing your HP. This makes armor the primary survival resource in a firefight — a player with high armor and average health is typically harder to kill than one with full health and no armor.

### Armor Items

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Armor Shard | Small AP pickup, found throughout maps |
| Green Armor | Moderate AP restore; lower cap |
| Yellow Armor | High AP restore; higher cap |

Each armor tier has a different maximum cap. Picking up armor beyond your current cap for that tier still grants AP up to the new cap, but you cannot mix-and-match to exceed what each type allows. Yellow Armor provides the most protection and is the highest-value spawn on most maps.

### Armor Bar

The armor bar on your HUD changes color based on your current AP level. This mirrors the health HUD — both give you at-a-glance status without demanding precise number-reading during combat.

## Item Timing and Map Control

Controlling health and armor spawns is the primary form of resource denial in Warfork. Items respawn on fixed timers. Experienced players track these timers mentally — they know when a Mega Health or Yellow Armor is about to respawn and position themselves to collect it while preventing the opponent from doing the same.

This is not just about getting pickups yourself. Denying your opponent a Mega Health when they're at 40 HP is often more decisive than healing yourself. Item control is map control. In Duel especially, the player who commands the major health and armor spawns dictates the pace of the match.
